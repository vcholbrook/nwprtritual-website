# NWPRT Visual Identity Reference

*Style guide for matching the NWPRT aesthetic on the Halo x NWPRT collab page and any other co-branded surfaces.*

---

## Brand essence

Newport Beach coastal heritage. Preppy headwear culture meets lifeguard-tower Americana. Confident, laid-back, location-anchored.

In one line: **Southern California heritage, stripped of its vowels and stamped with a trademark.**

The brand leans hard on place (Newport Beach, 22nd Street, The Wedge, Lido, Blackies, the jetty) and on heritage framing (Icon, Legacy, Classic, The One and Only). It is not loud, not hype-driven, not trend-chasing. It reads like a regional institution that happens to make very good hats.

## Typography

NWPRT uses three distinct type roles. Matching the roles matters more than matching the exact fonts.

### Display / wordmark

- **Style:** Condensed bold sans, all caps, tight letter-spacing.
- **Free font pulls:** Oswald, Bebas Neue, Anton, League Gothic.
- **Always stamped with the ™ mark.** Treat the wordmark like a stamped good, not a floating logo.

### Headline

- **Style:** Clean modern sans, medium weight (500), sentence case.
- **Free font pulls:** Inter, Helvetica Now, Söhne, IBM Plex Sans.
- Used for section titles and product names.

### Accent / editorial

- **Style:** Italic serif at small to mid size.
- **Free font pulls:** Playfair Display Italic, EB Garamond Italic, Libre Caslon Italic.
- Used for category labels ("Shop all brand store items", "Shop all truckers"). This is a quiet NWPRT tell that most people miss. Easy borrow.

### Body

- Same neutral sans as headlines, regular weight (400).
- Short sentences. Plain language. Rarely more than ten words per line.

## Visual vocabulary

### Photography

- Product on clean white for catalog shots.
- Lifestyle in natural daylight at the beach, jetty, pier, or harbor.
- Warm, unfiltered, slightly desaturated. Never over-retouched.
- People wearing product, not modeling it.

### Layout

- Generous whitespace. Nothing cramped.
- Grid-based product tiles, usually 3 or 4 across.
- Section dividers that breathe. Lots of vertical rhythm.
- Headers sit left-aligned with matching italic serif subheads.

### Iconography and marks

- The ™ stamp on the wordmark is part of the aesthetic, not a legal artifact.
- Round embroidered patches.
- Pill-shaped badges.
- Simple geometric shapes, never ornate.

### Surfaces

- Flat colors only. No gradients, no drop shadows, no glow effects.
- Texture comes from photography of fabric, foam, rope, and corduroy, not from CSS.
- Rounded corners are moderate. Nothing overly soft or playful.

## Practical notes for Halo x NWPRT

### The thread to pull on

The easiest way to make a Halo page feel part of the NWPRT world without copying them:

1. **Anchor the page in navy and cream.** Not navy and white. The cream is doing more work than it looks like.
2. **Reserve patch yellow for exactly one hero accent per view.** A badge, a horizontal rule, a CTA button, a price tag. If yellow shows up twice on the same screen, it loses its status as a signature.
3. **Run display type in a condensed all-caps sans.** This is the single most distinctive NWPRT visual move. Pairing a Halo headline with an Oswald/Bebas display treatment immediately reads co-branded.
4. **Use italic serif for category labels.** Small move, big tonal carry. "Shop the kit", "Read the story", "What's included" all work well in italic serif above a sans-serif headline.
5. **Let photography do the emotional work.** Avoid illustrated icons, gradients, or decorative CSS. A single photograph of a patch, a hat on a railing, or a bottle on sand carries more than any design flourish.

### Things to avoid

- Pure white backgrounds. Use cream or off-white.
- Drop shadows, glows, or gradients of any kind.
- Sans-serif italic. The italic must be serif.
- Stacking yellow on yellow or running it through large areas. It is a signature color, not a field color.
- Aspirational or hype copy. NWPRT never says "unlock", "elevate", "transform". Halo shouldn't either when sharing this thread.

### Co-branded lockup guidance

When placing the Halo and NWPRT marks together:

- Treat both marks as stamped goods. No floating, no glow, no drop shadow.
- Equal visual weight. Neither mark dominates.
- Separator should be a simple vertical rule or a small "x" in the shared type face, not a plus sign or ampersand.
- Keep both marks on a single flat surface, either navy or cream.
